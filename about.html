about me

what i've done

I am a creative director and design strategist working in sunny Portland, OR. I started my career as a graphic designer and Flash developer. As my career has evolved I have had many roles within user experience: information architect, content strategist, experience designer, front-end developer, brand strategist, and creative director.

I maintain a strong, formal design sensibility alongside a love for how things are built, how we manage that building process, and how we keep it all centered on the user.

I have a penchant for accessibility.
My favorite number is 508.
I make bad jokes.

what interests me

After working with Agile teams in order to incorporate engaging design, I’ve found that I am interested in that sweet spot where iterative development marries with a beautifully executed UCD.

As UX, especially within mobile, matures, users are less enticed by a feature-centric development approach and want more quality, more value, and a better quality experience. That requires a rethinking and retooling of the development and design philosophies.

  • How can we ensure UX and “big design” iterate successfully within the Agile environment?
  • Can haveing a CPSO that understands the full breadth of user experience help facilitate a strong project output (sucessful MVP) and strong project outcome (successful and usable design)? How valuable is it to the Agile process to have a CPSO that understands and advocates for a strong design?
